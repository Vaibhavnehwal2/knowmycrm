# Salesforce Adoption: It's Alignment, Not Features

Salesforce implementation is not about the features; it's about alignment on how to run the business within it. Successful adoption hinges on addressing key questions:

- **Who owns data quality?**
- **What are the daily non-negotiables for representatives?**
- **What aspects are standard versus flexible across teams?**
- **What serves as the system of truth (ERP, DMS, PLM, Excel)?**
- **How do we measure adoption without resorting to policing?**

CRM encompasses behavior, governance, and incentives, going beyond mere implementation.

---

## What's Your #1 Adoption Blocker?

Is it **people**, **process**, or **data**?

Understanding your primary blocker is the first step toward solving adoption challenges. Each requires a different approach:

- **People:** Focus on change management, training, and incentive alignment
- **Process:** Simplify workflows, reduce friction, and align CRM with actual work patterns
- **Data:** Establish clear ownership, quality standards, and integration with source systems
